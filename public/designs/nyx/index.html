Primordial · Cosmogony · The Eternal Night

From the
Void,
Night arose.

Before Gaia, before Eros, before the ordered cosmos — Nyx stepped forth from the primordial Chaos. She is the mother of a thousand darknesses: Sleep and Death, Dreams and Discord, Fate and Retribution. Feared even by Zeus.

Theogony · Hesiod ✦ c. 700 BCE ✦ Primordial Generation
✦ The Sovereign Domains
What Night governs,
she governs absolutely.
Domain I
Sleep
Ὕπνος · Hypnos
Twin of Death, born in a single night. Hypnos rules the nightly surrender of consciousness — the velvet erasure through which every mortal passes, and from which they return, diminished by a small death.
Domain II
Death
Θάνατος · Thanatos
Gentle and merciless in equal measure. Thanatos does not rage — he arrives. Unlike his violent sister Ker, his touch is the quiet extinguishing of a candle held too long against the wind.
Domain III
Dreams
Μορφεύς · Morpheus
Of all the Oneiroi, Morpheus alone can take the shape of a human figure — appearing to kings as counsellors, to widows as their lost husbands, to heroes as the gods themselves.
Domain IV
Fate
Μοῖραι · Moirai
Three sisters — Clotho who spins the thread, Lachesis who measures, Atropos whose name means unturnable. Even Zeus does not overturn their decree. They were there before he was.
Domain V
Discord
Ἔρις · Eris
Born of night, sister to strife. Her apple inscribed "to the fairest" — thrown at the wedding of Peleus — set in motion the chain of causation that required an entire city to burn.
Domain VI
Retribution
Νέμεσις · Nemesis
The proportioner of fortune. What is given in excess, she takes back. She is the corrective principle in a cosmos that tends toward equilibrium — the shadow behind every windfall.
✦ Study · The Shaper
Morpheus
Plate I · The Shaper of Dreams
Morpheus
Lord of Forms · Shaper of the Dream
Son of Hypnos ✦ Grandson of Nyx ✦ Chief of the Oneiroi

Of the thousand children of Sleep who inhabit the cave beyond the world's edge — where the sun never reaches and poppies grow in perpetual bloom — Morpheus alone holds the gift of the human form. His brothers, Phobetor and Phantasos, take the shapes of beasts and inanimate matter in sleep. It is Morpheus who steps into dreams wearing the face of someone you know.

He appears to kings in the guise of trusted counsellors; to grieving widows as the shade of their husbands; to heroes as the gods themselves. When Iris descends to his cave bearing a message from Olympus, she crosses the shore of Lethe, passes through the valley of perpetual dusk, and wakes him from the poppy-scented dark.

"There the god of sleep has his cave, deep among hollow mountains — a place the sun never touches with its beams, where night forever broods."

Ovid · Metamorphoses XI · c. 8 CE

The cave of the Oneiroi sits at the shore of Lethe, the river of forgetting. Every dream flows from this source: formed by Morpheus, delivered through the gate of horn if it is true, through ivory if it is false. He governs neither fate nor death — only the intermediate, the brief republic of the unconscious where the laws of daylight are suspended.

✦ Complete Progeny
The Children of Nyx,
as recorded by Hesiod
Moros
Μόρος · Doom
The impending sense of doom, distinct from death itself. The weight that descends before the end arrives.
Ker
Κήρ · Violent Death
The goddess of violent death, distinct from Thanatos's peaceful passage. She presides over the battlefield and sudden catastrophe.
Hypnos
Ὕπνος · Sleep
Twin of Thanatos. He makes his home in a cave of perpetual dusk, where the poppy grows and the river Lethe murmurs.
Thanatos
Θάνατος · Death
The personification of peaceful death. Unlike his sister Ker, he is depicted as a beautiful youth with an inverted torch.
Oneiroi
Ὄνειροι · The Dreams
A collective of dream-spirits — Morpheus, Phobetor, Phantasos — who govern the different categories and qualities of dream.
Momos
Μῶμος · Blame
The spirit of satire and criticism. He found fault with every creation and was eventually expelled from Olympus for mocking the gods.
Oizys
Ὀϊζύς · Misery
The spirit of woe and suffering, anxiety without cause or cure. She is often paired with Ponos, the toil that never ends.
Hesperides
Ἑσπερίδες · The Evening
The nymphs of evening, guardians of the garden at the world's western edge where the golden apples grow.
Moirai
Μοῖραι · The Fates
Clotho, Lachesis, and Atropos. They spin, measure, and cut the thread of life. Zeus himself does not overturn their decree.
Keres
Κῆρες · Death-Spirits
Female spirits of violent or cruel death, disease, old age, and the specific violence that attends battle.
Nemesis
Νέμεσις · Retribution
She redistributes fortune, bringing consequences on those who offend the cosmos with hubris or unearned excess.
Apate
Ἀπάτη · Deceit
The goddess of fraud and guile — present wherever self-deception, false witness, or deliberate dishonesty takes root.
Philotes
Φιλότης · Friendship
The spirit of affection and friendship, born alongside Discord — the ancient pairing of connection and its perpetual undoing.
Geras
Γῆρας · Old Age
The spirit of old age, who robs the body of strength and the eye of clarity. Depicted as a small, withered figure.
Eris
Ἔρις · Discord
Her uninvited apple — inscribed "to the fairest" — thrown at Peleus's wedding, set in motion the chain that ended Troy.
Styx
Στύξ · Hatred
The goddess of the underworld river, by which the gods swear their most binding oaths — oaths not even Zeus dares break.
